The Social Dilemma

  • Arbaz
  • Tech Blogs
  • 14

e are well acquainted with the advantages of social websites. It provides us a lot of privileges that some decades ago, people would not believe how much we will be able to reach the climax of productivity in coeval days. But we often overlook the other side of the coin. we are reached at a threshold where we have to take action or rather we are in the control of algorithms that are designed with the help of psychologists. And use our vulnerabilities to manipulate human actions and behaviors. We have a lot of information, tons of data, researches, and thousands of e-books. Everything is in our hands. We click and after a couple of minutes product reach at our home doors. We unite with our family, friends, and beloved makes communication way easier.

But what use of this, if we are totally under the control of these gadgets, can't control our actions, behavior, and controlled by the complex algorithms.

There are so many HD movies on the web today you would need 47 million years to watch them all. There are over 1.8 billion websites out there, and on average 571 new ones are created every minute. That’s how much data we’ve generated. It’s no wonder that information overload has become an issue.

A survey of 1011 Swedish Facebook users concludes that “the amount of time spent on Facebook had no relationship with self-esteem”

“When the entire population diet is FB: democracy crumbles”

More than 84% of the population has experience of putting off those all-important must-do tasks: which lead to procrastination.

“We like our internet to tell us what we want to hear”

Researches show that our processing power increases a trillion times after 1960. And our brains, psychology have evolved not at all. We can find more contented people in the 16th century than now regardless we have digital pacifiers.

If we have to compromise our mental health for external ornaments and luxuries than I think we are in trouble. Twitter Experts said:

"Everything you are doing online, is being watched, is being tracked, is being measured" Every single action you take is carefully mentioned and recorded. Exactly on what image you stop and look at, and for how long you look at it.

"if you are not paying for the product than you are the product"

Of course, they are not selling you the product without your consent but all they want to do is "its the gradual, slight, imperceptible change in your own behavior and perception that is the product" That's all they want a slight change in behavior towards certain products. They inculcate information about products so meticulously that changed our behavior without consent of ourselves and use our psychology against us. There is nothing to sell but changing what you do, how you think, who you are, in terms of unrealistic standards of beauty that are inculcated in us. They have more information about us then we had ourselves. The question is what do they do with all the data?

They build models that predict actions. The more you spent time on sites the more accurate the model will be. We have been programmed at a deeper level. They designed algorithms so well that the recommendations videos are exactly according to your previous interest and you have to click it.

“Google doesn’t have a proxy for the truth that's better than clicks”

We are training our young generation that if they feel lonely, uncomfortable, or uncertain we have a digital pacifier. AI can anticipate your next move you know nothing about.

Everybody thinks they are right and others are stupid they don't want to socialize with people who have opposite opinions and this attitude creates polarization which ultimately leads to civil violence. Look at different sects in our country how people's opinions are diametrically opposed to each other.

Fake news spread 6 times faster. Because the truth is boring. if you want to control the population of a country there is never been a useful tool as Facebook says, experts. we lost ourselves we become what others tell us to be. These all cause political polarization, social stratification, self-esteem issues anti-social behavior, self-harm in teens, suicide attempts, alienation, and loss of internal contentment.
